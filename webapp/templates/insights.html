What is SHAP?

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is a game-theoretic approach to explain machine learning predictions. It calculates the contribution of each feature to the final prediction.

Shows which factors increase or decrease risk
Provides individual explanations for each prediction
Mathematically consistent and fair attribution

Loan Risk Factors 101

Understanding the factors that affect your loan approval

Credit Score

Your credit score reflects your creditworthiness. Higher scores indicate responsible credit management and lower default risk.

Excellent: 750+
Good: 700-749
Fair: 650-699
Poor: <650< /div>

DTI Ratio

Debt-to-Income ratio measures your monthly debt payments against your income. Lower ratios indicate better ability to manage new debt.

💡 Lenders prefer DTI ratios below 43% for loan approval.

Employment Stability

Longer employment duration indicates income stability, reducing lender risk. Full-time employment is generally preferred.

💡 2+ years at current job significantly improves approval chances.

Income Level

Higher income provides more financial cushion to handle loan payments, reducing default probability.

💡 Stable, verifiable income is more important than absolute amount.

Education

Higher education levels often correlate with better earning potential and financial literacy, improving loan repayment odds.

Co-Signer

Having a co-signer with good credit can significantly reduce loan risk, as they share repayment responsibility.

💡 Co-signers can help secure better interest rates and terms.
